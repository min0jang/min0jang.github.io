Reinforcement Learning on Car control

The purpose of this project is to apply reinforcement learning process on a simulated car to autonomously drive toward the flag. After simulation, the trained model will be applied on an actual small-sized car.

This project is forArtificial Intelligence Driving Olympics, participating as a part of Cornell Autonomous Systems Lab.

2-D movement

Goal is to have the car learn to reach the flag in the shortest amount time possible. In each episode, the car is generated at a point in the left 1/3 of the space. The inputs of the Reinforcement Learning models are: (x,y) of car, (x,y) of goal, theta, speed.

The goal is to have the simulated car to learn how to go right while getting as close as possible to the flag.

When the car reaches the x-position of the flag, each episode ends. However, the reward is calculated based on the y-position of the car--how far it was in each step from the flag. In each step, the car randomly chooses an action for its velocity and direction.

In the beginning of each episode, the car is generated at a random point on the left side of the space. Each episode is comprised of tens to thousands of steps. In each step, the car randomly chooses an action for its velocity and direction. Based on the reward calculated in each action, the car "reinforcement learn" how to drive toward the flag.

There are two types of actions: change in speed and change in direction.

For speed, it can decelerate, stay, or accelerate.

For direction, it can turn left, stay, or turn right.
