Reinforcement Learning on Car control

The purpose of this project is to apply reinforcement learning process on a simulated car to autonomously drive toward the flag. After simulation, the trained model will be applied on an actual small-sized car.

This project is forArtificial Intelligence Driving Olympics, participating as a part of Cornell Autonomous Systems Lab.

1-D Linear movement

Goal is to have the car learn to reach the flag in the shortest amount time possible. It is designed to STOP at the flag location, but I was quite generous with the threshold for velocity to speed up the learning process--otherwise, it takes too long for each episode.

2-D movement

Now, I added y-axis to the simulation. In each episode, the car is generated at a random point on the left 1/3 of the space. The inputs of the Reinforcement Learning models are: x-position, y-position, theta, speed and the ouputs were theta_new and speed_new.

The reason the car hits the bottom-left corner every time is because the coordinates of the flag was not given to the learning model, and the car is generated randomly every time. It is reasonable for the model to decide to reach a certain point (which was bottom-left point here) and make a diagonal movement toward the flag.
